Hackers

Hackers

A hacker is someone who tries to breach or exploit a weakness in a computer system or network. There are many different types of hackers, some do it ethically as a job and others do it maliciously.

White Hat Hacker

A white hat hacker is a ethical hacker. They are often known as penetration testers because companies pay them to break into websites and fix the weaknesses. They focus on securing the system and because of this, this is the only version of hacking that is legal.

Black Hat Hacker

A black hat hacker is the opposite of a white hat hacker. They are unethical hackers. They hack into systems to steal money or for their own agenda. An axample of a black hat hacker would be the hacking group Anonymous. The illegally hack into systems for their own goals.

Grey Hat Hacker

Grey hat hackers are a somewhat mix between white hat and black hat hackers. They hack into websites without permission not to steal money or change the content of the system but to find weaknesses. They will usally report it to the owner of the website for free or ask for money. Most of the time what they do is considered illegal.

Hacking Groups

Hacking groups are when a group of hackers work together to achieve a goal. By working together they are a lot more effective and can cause a lot more damage. A group known as Anonymous are the most well known hacker group and they mainly target groups they view as corrupt. Governments are now also creating their own hacker groups which they use in cyber warfare.
